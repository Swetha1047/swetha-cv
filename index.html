Work Experience

NASA Robotics Systems Technology Intern – Clear Lake, TX (Jun 2024 – Aug 2024)

During the course of this ten-week internship at the Johnson Space Center, I helped advance the Lunar Terrain Vehicle's solar-power station from the conceptual stage to field-hardware readiness. I redesigned the entire assembly in CAD, built low- and high-fidelity prototypes, and completed a full electronics upgrade, installing a new inverter, batteries, and charge controller before conducting verification. At the same time, I re-engineered the Space Exploration Vehicle (SEV): retracing all wire runs, replacing bad connectors, and creating a master wiring guide for future interns. My final project was refurbishing SEV transmissions, manufacturing replacement parts, lubricating gear trains, and reassembling the units to function testing criteria, all of which restored vehicles to readiness for future lunar-simulation tests.
